  • Market inflection: The broader AI video market is growing at a 32.2% CAGR, from $3.86B in 2024 to a projected $42.29B by 2033, driven by enterprise content automation, creator tools, and a 342% year-over-year increase in adoption.

  • Elo vs. structured benchmarks diverge: Runway Gen-4.5 leads community Elo rankings (1,247) while scoring differently on structured benchmarks, suggesting aesthetic appeal factors beyond raw technical quality drive user preference.

Exhibit 10
Three structural shifts will define the next 12 months: architecture convergence, price commoditization, and the audio-visual integration race
01
Architecture Convergence on DiT
Diffusion Transformer variants have become the consensus backbone. Sustainable differentiation now requires training data moats or novel architectural extensions — Seedance's dual-branch audio-visual co-generation and Wan2.2's MoE denoising specialization represent the current frontier of innovation.

Implication: Pure architecture is no longer a moat. Data quality and scale will determine winners.
02
Price Commoditization Accelerating
Per-clip costs will compress toward $0.30–0.50 as compute costs decline and open-source models (Wan2.2, LTX-2) exert downward pressure. Veo 3.1's premium pricing ($2.50/clip) will become increasingly difficult to sustain without clear value differentiation.

Implication: Monetization will shift from generation fees to workflow tools, enterprise APIs, and content management platforms.
03
Audio-Visual Co-Generation Becomes Table Stakes
Native audio generation (pioneered by Seedance 2.0's dual-branch approach, matched by Veo 3.1) will become table stakes by mid-2027. Models without synchronized audio will be relegated to prototyping and draft use cases.

Implication: The competitive frontier is shifting from "can it generate video?" to "can it generate production-ready audiovisual content?"
Bottom line: The era of "which AI is best?" is over. In 2026, production teams use multi-model strategies — Seedance for template-based work and multimodal remixing, Kling for rapid prototyping, Sora 2 or Veo 3.1 for final high-quality deliverables, and Wan2.2 for privacy-sensitive local generation.
Source: Analyst synthesis based on market data, technical benchmarks, and pricing trends, February 2026
